# Practitioner Lessons (First-Person-Informed)

This file distills process lessons from public practitioner accounts and project retrospectives.

## 1) Samba (Tridgell / Allison)

- Wire-level observation plus iterative implementation worked in practice.
- Early legal boundary decisions mattered as much as technical choices.
- Refusing NDA channels incompatible with open publication prevented later lock-in.

Representative source:
- Linux Journal interview (1998): https://www.linuxjournal.com/article/2900

## 2) ReactOS contamination controversy and audits

- Even one contributor disclosure of disassembly exposure can trigger prolonged trust and provenance damage.
- Projects that survive this move from policy slogans to explicit audits, external review, and recorded legal bases.

## 3) Portable takeaways

1. Team wall design is architecture, not paperwork.
2. Provenance evidence is a primary deliverable.
3. Tests are both engineering quality control and legal defensibility infrastructure.
4. Incident drills should happen before incidents.
